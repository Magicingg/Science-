WORLD
ECOSYSTEM:
FOREST
The forest ecosystem is a dynamic system of trees, plants, animals, fungi, and microorganisms interacting with their environment. It includes tropical, temperate, boreal, and mangrove forests, each supporting diverse life. Forests absorb carbon dioxide, produce oxygen, regulate the water cycle, and prevent soil erosion. They provide wildlife habitats, sustain biodiversity, and support human livelihoods through timber, medicinal plants, and ecotourism. However, deforestation, climate change, and pollution threaten them. Conservation efforts like reforestation, responsible management, and global initiatives are crucial for preserving biodiversity, and securing resources for future generations.
WORLD
ECOSYSTEM:
TUNDRA
Cold ecosystems in polar regions and high altitudes challenge life with frigid temperatures, limited sunlight, and short growing seasons. Organisms adapt through insulation, hibernation, migration, and specialized diets. These ecosystems showcase biodiversity and natural selection. However, climate change, melting ice, and human activities threaten them. Conservation, climate action, and sustainable land use are vital to preserving their wildlife and balance.
WORLD
ECOSYSTEM:
MARINE
The ocean ecosystem, covering over 70% of Earth, supports diverse marine life and regulates climate, produces oxygen, and provides food and livelihoods. Key habitats include open oceans, coral reefs, deep-sea trenches, coastal waters, and polar seas. Threats like pollution, overfishing, and climate change endanger ocean health. Conservation efforts, including marine reserves, sustainable fishing, and pollution reduction, are vital for preserving biodiversity and resources.

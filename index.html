SAVING OUR

ECOSYSTEM

An ecosystem is a geographic area where biotic factors (plants, animals, and other organisms) and abiotic factors (weather and landscapes) interact to form a balanced system of life, within an area.

Why save our ECOSYSTEM?

Our ecosystem keeps us alive by providing clean air, fresh water, food, and a stable climate. When we harm nature, we risk our health, resources, and future. Protecting ecosystems means protecting ourselves. Everyone needs nature.

Clean Air & Water

Ecosystems filter air and water, giving us fresh oxygen and clean drinking water. Destroying them leads to pollution and health risks.

Food & Resources

Healthy ecosystems provide food, medicine, and materials we use daily. Without them, food shortages and resource depletion become serious problems.

Climate Protection

Forests and oceans absorb carbon dioxide, helping regulate the climate. Without them, extreme weather and global warming worsen.
